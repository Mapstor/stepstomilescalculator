Before You Contact Us

You might find your answer here without needing to wait for a reply:

"Is this calculator accurate?"

The calculator uses scientifically-based formulas to estimate stride length from your height (walking stride = height × 0.415, running stride = height × 0.52). These are population averages that work well for most people. For the most accurate results, you can measure your actual stride length and enter it as a custom value. See our About page for detailed methodology information.

"Can I use this calculator on my website?"

Linking to our calculator is always welcome. If you'd like to embed the calculator directly on your website, please email us first to discuss options and get permission.

"I found a bug"

Thank you for helping us improve! When reporting a bug, please include:

  • Browser and version (e.g., Chrome 120, Safari 17, Firefox 121)
  • Device type (e.g., iPhone 14, Windows laptop, Android tablet)
  • Steps to reproduce the issue
  • What happened vs. what you expected

"I have a feature request"

We welcome suggestions for new features or improvements. When submitting a feature request, it helps to explain the use case: what are you trying to accomplish, and how would this feature help?

Popular Questions

"How many miles is 10,000 steps?"

For most adults, 10,000 steps equals approximately 4–5 miles depending on height. Our complete 10K steps guide breaks down the exact distance by height.

"What's the formula for steps to miles?"

The formula is: Miles = (Steps × Stride Length in Feet) ÷ 5,280. See our steps to miles formula guide for detailed explanations and examples.

"How many steps are in a mile?"

Approximately 2,000–2,500 steps per mile when walking, depending on your height. See our steps in a mile guide for precise numbers.
